ECO-ESCORT: Data driven carbon emission reduction

Introduction

Our names are Andy Yan, Jack Sullivan, Jesse Zhang, Kyle Tse, Simon Behar, John Leyden and Vasudev Venkatesh and our supervisors are PhD candidate K. Shankari and Professor David Culler. We are undergraduates at the University of California, Berkeley, in the department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. We are planning to conduct a research study, which we invite you to take part in.

Purpose

The purpose of this study is to influence user behavior towards more sustainable methods of transportation and to analyze which set of features result in the most net positive carbon emission impact.

What we collect

If you agree to participate in our research, we will automatically track your location, accelerometer, device-generated activity recognition, and battery usage. We will also allow you to optionally enter your experiences and feedback during travel through the app. Furthermore, there will be an initial survey upon first launch of the application to collect information about your age, socioeconomic status, and health status. This information will never be made public, unless you specifically opt-in to releasing your data after the study (with all personal identifiers removed i.e. email, age, socioeconomic status), and is for research purposes only. Since our code is open source, you can inspect the data that we collect in the background at https://github.com/e-mission/e-mission-data-collection.git, explore the prompts for optional sentiment reporting at https://github.com/e-mission/e-mission-phone.git, and see the analysis that we perform at https://github.com/e-mission/e-mission-server.git

How we associate your data with you

You will log in to the app using a valid email address. We map the email address to a UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) and associate all collected data with the UUID. In general, the mapping between the email address and the UUID will only be made available to the researchers of the platform. If you are not comfortable sharing your real email address, you can update your email or create an address specifically for the use of this experiment. The only reason we use an email address is so that we can ensure that your data is only available to somebody logged in as you, and remains available even if you switch phones.

Who gets to see it

Individually identified, real-time data is only available to the lead researchers. It will not be used for marketing or advertising purposes. Aggregate views of the collected data (both automatic and optional) that do not show individual information (e.g. heatmaps) will be made publicly available through the platform website and on the phone app. Some of your aggregated trip information may get displayed to other users within the app. These trip statistics over certain time periods include total carbon emissions, change of emission amount from last time, total distance and time traveled, and favorite mode of transportation. Hence, no personal, identifiable information will be available to any other user of the application.

Benefits

Benefits for participating in this study include providing you with a personalized travel profile with state and federal goals for daily exercise and GHG emission reduction.

Compensation

There will be a drawing of ten $50 Amazon gift cards randomly given out to participants. The submission of your email address in the initial survey you filled out will be your entry into this drawing. You will receive a compensation benefit with a probability value of 10 divided by the number of total participants.

Risks and Discomforts

There may be some discomfort regarding filling out the initial survey, however the survey data is secured on a E-Mission platform server that only the researchers have access to. There may also be some discomfort about being tracked, however the study location data will also be secured through said server. There is a miniscule but undeniably present risk regarding a data breach, but we are taking precautions to minimize this risk.

Confidentiality

Your study data will be handled as confidentially as possible. If the results of this study are published or presented, individual names and other personally identifiable information will not be used. To minimize risks to confidentiality, we will store study data on a secure, encrypted E-Mission platform server. The server can only be accessed by the researchers and nobody else.

Your rights

Participation in research is completely voluntary. You have the right to decline to participate or to withdraw at any point in this study without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.
